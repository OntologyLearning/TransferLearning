Interests

I use an integrative approach to cardiac electrophysiology research, combining clinical and experimental data, modelling and simulation, to investigate cardiac arrhythmias and possible therapeutic targets. Translation of results from basic science to clinical and industrial applications is central to my work via collaboration with clinicians and industrial partners. My current research interests focus on the improved modelling of cardiac conduction, variability, characterization of substrates for ventricular arrhythmias, signalling pathways in atrial fibrillation, and inflammatory response during myocardial ischemia.

Biography

I obtained a degree in Industrial Engineer and a PhD in Physics and Mathematics from the Universidad de Castilla-La Mancha, Spain, in 2002 and 2007, respectively. After completion of my PhD studies, I spent two years working in industry, in the development of telecommunication networks and as Research & Development Manager. This was followed by a Postdoctoral position at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, under a research initiative funded by AIRBUS Operations for advanced shape optimisation applied to aeronautical design. In September 2010, I joined the Computational Biology Group at the Department of Computer Science at Oxford, as a Postdoctoral Research Assistant in Computational Cardiac Electrophysiology with Dr. Blanca Rodriguez, funded by the preDiCT project and later by Dr. Rodriguez’ MRC award. In February 2012 I joined the Oxford Collaborative Centre for Applied Mathematics, for the development of innovative frameworks for the description of electrical propagation in cardiac tissue. Now I'm back to the Department of Computer Science, where I continue collaborating with Dr. Blanca Rodriguez funded by her Wellcome Trust Senior Research Fellowship in Biomedical Sciences.
